More About Me

I have over 5 years of programming experience in both academic and industrial contexts, primarly in Python Java, and C++. I have also used HTML, CSS, and React.js to create front-end web applications for company and personal projects. Currently, I'm studying Data Structures, Algorithms, Object-Oriented Programming, Data Analysis, and Discrete Math, all which are required for my major.

I'm also a part of Robomasters Ultraviolet: NYU's robotics team that designs three robots geared towards competive play (notable adversaries include UW's Advanced Robotics and Texas A&M's Aimbot Robotics teams). Each year, three robots (known as the Standard, Hero and Sentry) are desiged that are capable of launching 17mm plastic projectiles at high speeds with significant accuracy. Specifically, I'm a part of the computer vision team, working on our fully autonomous robot (the Sentry). We use Intel's RealSense camera, OpenCV, and YOLOv5 (running on a Jetson Nano) for real-time object detection. Geometric transformations are used to calculate the distance and angle of the target, which is then used to control the robot's movement and firing speed.

When I'm not coding, I enjoy photography, the oudoors, and being active in any way possible (rock climbing, hitting the gym, Ultimate Frisbee ... the list could go on for a while). I also share a project car (a 2001 Mazda Miata MX-5 NB) with a couple friends from my hometown. Currently, we've installed a removable steering wheel, cold air intake, an upgraded exhaust that can switch to and from straight piped at the press of a key fob, and custom fitted bucket seats. Hopefully, we can turbo it soon and get it on a track!
